Photo by Robbie Shone @shonephoto | Austria's University of Innsbruck is one of the main institutions carrying out research to better understand the past, present, and future of European ice caves. Pictured are some of the world’s leading young scientists in this field: Maria Wind, Tanguy Racine, and Gabriella Koltai study maps and reports on ice caves they've sampled. In "When the Magic Melts," my article in the October issue of the magazine, we document the contributions of scientists—and tour guides—as they work against the clock in this rapidly disappearing landscape.
