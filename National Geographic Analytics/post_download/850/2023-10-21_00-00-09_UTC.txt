Photo by @jaimerojo | At the beginning of fall 2023, sea ice in Antarctica hit a record low. Ice-dependent species, like these Adelie penguins in Paulet Island, off the northeastern end of the Antarctic Peninsula, will face new hardships to feed and breed as the extent of sea ice cover shrinks. Their population is expected to drop drastically on a warmer planet. Follow @jaimerojo for more pictures and stories about the last wild places on Earth.
