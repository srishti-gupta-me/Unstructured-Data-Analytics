Photo by Keith Ladzinski @ladzinski | On the exposed plains of Tornado Alley—a vertical swath of the central United States that's prone to the storms—one can get caught in the crosshairs of harsh weather throughout much of the year. A small herd of horses restlessly dances under the dark clouds of a supercell storm over southern New Mexico. It wasn't long before heavy rain and hail began to fall, and with nowhere to run, the only option was to simply take what nature brings.
