Peru is currently experiencing one of the worst dengue outbreaks recorded with more than 170,000 Peruvians suffering from the mosquito-borne virus and more than 225 dead. The outbreak is driven in part by unusually wet conditions caused by the climate phenomenon known as El Niño. While scientists believe the impact of El Niño cycles are increasingly exacerbated by climate change, archaeological evidence shows the climate phenomenon has been severely affecting the region for more than a thousand years, with past societies battling its effects with practical on-the-ground engineering as well as spiritual appeals to the gods—appeals that have included acts of ritual child sacrifice unprecedented in world history.
