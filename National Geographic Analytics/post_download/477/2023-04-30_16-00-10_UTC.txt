Photo by @paoloverzone | Treasures from the past increase with every excavation, including at the archaeological site of Heit el Ghurab, a 4,500-year-old Egyptian city long considered the home of workers who built the Pyramids at Giza. The new Grand Egyptian Museum, also located close to the famous monuments, celebrates the country’s rich cultural heritage by preserving and showcasing the country’s wealth of artifacts, including objects from Tutankhamun’s tomb.
