Photo by @amivitale | Najma studies by candlelight in the single room she shares with her 10 siblings in Kenya's Loita Hills area.  She passed exams necessary to obtain entrance to a county high school. When Kenya enacted a law that made primary education free, enrollment rates increased to 84 percent. However, only one in five girls make it to their eighth year. This high dropout rate is a result of early marriage, poverty, and other factors. Investing in girls' education has profound impacts.
