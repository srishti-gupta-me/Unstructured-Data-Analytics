Death Valley holds the record for hottest air temperature ever recorded, a whopping 134°F (about 56°C) in 1913. But it wasn’t named after its scorching summer temps—it actually got its name from a winter disaster in 1849. The few settlers who made it out alive assigned the haunting moniker.
