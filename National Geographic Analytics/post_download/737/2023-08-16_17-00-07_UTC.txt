Bears know when it’s trash day, coyotes learned to look both ways before crossing the street, and raccoons can untie bungee cords. As more scientists study the creatures right under our noses, a consistent message is emerging: Many species are adapting to urban life in unprecedented ways. It’s a phenomenon happening in urban areas around the world. The more we understand the animals living in our midst, ecologists say, the better we can get along with these urban newcomers.
