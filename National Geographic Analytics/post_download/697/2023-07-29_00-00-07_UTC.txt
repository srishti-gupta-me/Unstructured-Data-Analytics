Two newly discovered objects are the source of pulses of radio waves that have arrived every 21 minutes for at least 33 years. They look like neutron stars—the dense stellar cores left behind after massive stars explode in supernovae—but they have other characteristics that astronomers can’t explain. They may even represent a new type of stellar object, unlike anything seen before.
