Photo by @nicholesobecki | Generally speaking, smaller animals have shorter life spans than larger ones, but bats break the rule. Eighteen of the 19 mammals that live proportionally longer than humans based on body size are bats. (The naked mole rat is the other.) This spectacularly colored yellow-winged bat, with soft gray fur and long, translucent ears, reminds me of how diverse these flying mammals are, with nearly 1,400 species living everywhere on the globe, except for extreme desert and polar regions.
