Despite a plethora of products designed to keep birds off buildings, they're sending a clear message: Nice try.

While birds are a common urban neighbor, city residents don’t always appreciate the droppings and nesting materials our feathered friends leave behind. To keep birds off human infrastructure, light displays, netting, and even sharp metal spikes were designed. However, a new study shows that not only are some birds unbothered by long strips of barbs—they’re actually ripping the skewers off buildings and using them to build armored nurseries for their chicks.
