Photo by Charlie Hamilton James @chamiltonjames |  Male sage grouse strut their stuff at dawn in the sagebrush west of the Wind River Range, in Wyoming. The male birds gather in the spring, assembling in leks, or courting arenas, where they display to females by fanning their tails and inflating their two olive-colored neck pouches that sit inside their impressive ruff. The sound accompanying their bizarre performance is a mix of gentle booming and popping noises, which, at daybreak in the cold of the high desert, makes for an eerie experience.
