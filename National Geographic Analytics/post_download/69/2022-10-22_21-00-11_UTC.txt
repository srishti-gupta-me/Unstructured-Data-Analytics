Photo by Robbie Shone @shonephoto | Words by National Geographic Explorer @denisehruby: Melting winter snow fills a depression in the Italian Dolomites where once there was a year-round lake. In 1994, Italian explorers discovered that the lake had vanished—because the ice plugging a hole at the bottom had melted, allowing the water to drain through a narrow shaft into a 935-foot-deep ice cave known as El Cenote. In the October 2022 "When the Magic Melts," my most recent article in National Geographic magazine, we document the important contributions of scientists—and tour guides—as they work against the clock in this rapidly disappearing landscape.
