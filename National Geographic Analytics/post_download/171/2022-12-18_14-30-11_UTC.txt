Photo by @estherhorvath | In an old military bunker, scientists trace the rise—and sometimes the fall—of chemical pollutants in an industrial country. 

Till Weber manages the German Environmental Specimen Bank, where cryo-storage tanks hold 400,000 vials of blood and urine, annually collected over four decades from German volunteers on behalf of the German Environment Agency. 

It's part of the federal ministry's effort to monitor exposure to chemicals like lead, mercury, and plasticizers. The project traces which substances have accumulated at potentially dangerous levels, and considers whether bans and regulations of some of those substances have been effective.
