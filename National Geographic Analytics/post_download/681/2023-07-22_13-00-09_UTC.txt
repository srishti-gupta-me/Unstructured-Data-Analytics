Photo by @javier_aznar_photography | A dazzling pair of jewel weevils (Eurhinus sp.) mate on a tendril on the slopes of the Tungurahua volcano in Ecuador. After mating, the female often uses its long rostrum to bore a hole into a plant, laying eggs inside so the larvae will hatch surrounded by food to eat.
