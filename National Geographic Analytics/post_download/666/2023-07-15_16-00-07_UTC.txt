The Sonoran Desert toad is increasingly being caught by poachers, for recreation and profit, for what the toads carry in their bodies—5-MeO-DMT, a powerful psychotropic compound. The allure of the toad for its psychoactive properties is complicated because 5-MeO-DMT is currently being studied as a possible treatment for depression and anxiety. As the popularity of 5-MeO-DMT rises, the question now is about the ethics of animal use—especially when there is an alternative synthetic version of the drug. Read more at the link in bio.
