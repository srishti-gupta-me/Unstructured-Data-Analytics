Photo by @williamodaniels | Rohingya refugees pray at a mosque under construction in the Balu Kali camp.

On August 25, 2017, a deadly crackdown had forced more than 700,000 Rohingya Muslims to flee their homes in Myanmar—where the majority of the population is Buddhist—in what the United Nations human rights chief at the time called “a textbook example of ethnic cleansing.” Most crossed the border into neighboring Bangladesh to take refuge in camps near Cox’s Bazar.
