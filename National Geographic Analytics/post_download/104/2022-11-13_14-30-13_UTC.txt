long-extinct creatures aren’t just for museums—they’re in homes and businesses, as wealthy collectors indulge a controversial hobby. At the Hanging Gardens of Marqueyssac in France, communications officer Stéphanie Angleys cleans the display case of a 150-million-year-old allosaurus. Owned by French businessman Kléber Rossillon, the gardens are open to the public. Rossillon acquired the dinosaur—whose skull is among the best preserved in the world—in 2016, a few years after it was found in Wyoming (U.S.).
