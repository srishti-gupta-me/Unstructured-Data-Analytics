Scientists are just beginning to learn how these common tumors in women are linked to phthalates—chemicals found in hundreds of everyday household items and cosmetics. These chemicals have been linked to uterine fibroids—non-cancerous tumors ranging from the size of a seed to a soccer ball that grow in or around the uterus.
