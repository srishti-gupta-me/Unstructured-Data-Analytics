In May 1845 a British explorer and naval officer, Sir John Franklin, took up the quest to find a route between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans through Arctic waters. With orders from the British Admiralty, Franklin and a crew of 133 sailed out from the Thames. The crew was young, tough, and seasoned. Both ships were specially equipped for polar service and carried more than three years’ worth of food and drink. Despite these preparations, the expedition failed—becoming one of the grimmest chapters in the history of Arctic exploration.
